Elephant

Elephants are the largest living land animals. Three living species are currently recognised: the African bush elephant (Loxodonta africana), the African forest elephant (L. cyclotis), and the Asian elephant (Elephas maximus). They are the only surviving members of the family Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea; extinct relatives include mammoths and mastodons. Distinctive features of elephants include a long proboscis called a trunk, tusks, large ear flaps, pillar-like legs, and tough but sensitive grey skin.

zebra

A zebra is a wild African animal known for its distinctive black and white striped coat. There are three species of zebra: the plains zebra, the mountain zebra, and Grevy's zebra. They are all members of the horse family, Equidae. Zebras are primarily found in various African habitats, including savannas, grasslands, and mountainous regions.

Kenguru

"Kenguru" can refer to two distinct things: a wheelchair-accessible electric vehicle and street workout/calisthenics equipment. The term itself is Hungarian for "kangaroo".
